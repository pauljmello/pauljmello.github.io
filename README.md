# Paul Jason Mello

PhD student in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Nevada, Reno, researching the mathematical foundations of deep learning. My work bridges information theory, category theory, and geometric methods to understand and improve how neural networks learn and generalize.

## Research Interests

- **Category Theory & Deep Learning**: Building learning frameworks from categorical first principles (Cartesian closed categories, lens composition, Para construction).
- **Information Theory**: Applying information-theoretic tools to analyze learning dynamics, generalization, and implicit biases in neural networks.
- **Geometric & Manifold Methods**: Studying model behavior through high-dimensional feature geometry, particularly under limited observational samples.
- **Generative Modeling**: Exploring diffusion processes, score-based objectives, and their connections to thermodynamic systems.

## Selected Projects

- **[CatDL — Categorical Deep Learning](https://github.com/pauljmello/CatDL-Categorical-Deep-Learning)**: A deep learning framework built from category theory, implementing MLPs, CNNs, and ResNets via Cartesian reverse differential categories.
- **[Random Matrix Theory](https://github.com/pauljmello/Random-Matrix-Theory)**: An exploration of random matrix theory utilizing Gaussian ensembles.
- **[Modern and Traditional Hopfield Networks](https://github.com/pauljmello/Hopfield-Networks)**: Implementing and comparing classical and modern Hopfield network capacity.
- **[Perturbation Driven Generalization](https://github.com/pauljmello/Perturbation-Driven-Generalizaion)**: Exploring the effects of noise across scales, architectures, intensities, and methods.
- **[Sheaf Neural Networks](https://github.com/pauljmello/Sheaf-Neural-Networks)**: Implementation of sheaf-theoretic neural networks.
- **[Echo State Networks](https://github.com/pauljmello/Echo-State-Networks)**: Reservoir computing implementation based on Jaeger (2001).

See [all projects](https://pauljmello.github.io/all-projects.html) for the full list.
