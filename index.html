About Me

Hi, I'm Paul! A PhD student at the University of Nevada, Reno researching artificial intelligence. My passion is extracting, optimizing, and generalizing the fundamentals of neural networks through information theory. Previously, I worked on uncovering entropic biases present in training data and mitigating them with corrective behaviors. More recently I have been engaging in projects on grokking, B-splines, and emergent phase transitions.

When I'm not researching, I enjoy biking, strategy games, and a good cup of chai. Feel free to reach out to me via email or any of my socials if you'd like to connect!
